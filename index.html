A Visual Trace Map of Edinburgh Place Names in Literature

MSc Dissertation prototypes — six interactive D3.js visualisations exploring narrative structure vs. geographic accuracy
Merritt Wang (S2887338) · University of Edinburgh, School of Informatics · supervised by Uta Hinrichs

Direction 1 — Author Spatial Fingerprints

Can each author's distribution of place names across Edinburgh form a distinct visual "fingerprint," recognisable without prior knowledge of their work?
Radar Chart
Primary design. Polygon shape across 14 official city sectors; click a vertex for places, books, and example sentences.
Bar-code Fingerprint
Secondary design. Per-place bars, independent Y-axis per author; sortable, searchable.
Small Multiples
Tertiary design. Five real Leaflet maps side by side; sector filter and 2-author compare mode.

Direction 2 — Narrative Topology Networks

Does document-level co-occurrence of place names reveal a narrative structure distinct from geographic proximity?
Force-Directed Network
Primary design. Node-link diagram of the full 403-pair co-occurrence graph; weight-threshold slider, click a node for details.
Linear Connection Diagram
Secondary design. Places on a single axis, arcs for co-occurrence; sortable, click an arc for the book list.
Metro-Style Map
Illustrative design. Lines derived from real community detection on the co-occurrence graph, not hand-drawn geography — 88% of adjacent stations share a real book co-occurrence.
All six visualisations above use the same five test authors (Alexander McCall Smith, Irvine Welsh, John Gibson Lockhart, Walter Scott, Robert Louis Stevenson) and are the exact versions described in the dissertation — each is a self-contained file with its data embedded, so nothing needs a server or an internet connection beyond loading D3.js/Leaflet.js from their CDNs. Click through, hover for tooltips, and click a node/vertex/bar/arc/station for a detail panel with an example sentence from the source text.
Combined Interface (framework scaffold) → a single-page shell navigating between all six visualisations above, with an author selector and cross-view linking planned as the next iteration.
Source: github.com/merrittw-1307/uoe-edinburgh-literary-geovis · A follow-up scale exploration (2 to 408 authors) is documented separately in data/processed/scale_exploration/ and the dissertation's Limitations chapter.
